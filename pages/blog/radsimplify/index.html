The problem:

Simplify the following: $$ \sqrt{9 - 6\sqrt{2}} + \sqrt{9 + 6\sqrt{2}} $$

My solution:

Let $ 9 + 6\sqrt{2} = c^2 $
Let $ 9 - 6\sqrt{2} = d^2 $
Then: $$ (c+d)^2 = c^2 + 2cd + d^2 \\ = c^2 + d^2 + 2cd \\ = 18 + 2cd \\ = 18 + 2(\sqrt{9 - 6\sqrt{2}})(\sqrt{9 + 6\sqrt{2}}) \\ = 18 + 2\sqrt{(9 - 6\sqrt{2})(9 + 6\sqrt{2})} \\ = 18 + 2\sqrt{81 - 72} \\ = 18 + 2\sqrt{9} \\ = 18 + 2(3) \\ = 18 + 6 \\ = 24 \\ = 2\sqrt{6} $$
